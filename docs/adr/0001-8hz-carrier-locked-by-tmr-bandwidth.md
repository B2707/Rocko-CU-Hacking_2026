# Carrier frequency, locked by the TMR sensor bandwidth

The team's TMR sensors are single axis, analog, and only respond usefully in
the 0 to 10 Hz band. Published through-the-earth systems run carriers from
300 Hz up to several kHz, but anything above roughly 10 Hz is invisible to
this hardware, so the carrier has to live inside that band.

The build settled on 8 Hz. This keeps the tone comfortably inside the
sensor's usable range while giving the bandpass filter and envelope detector
on the surface station a clean, narrow band to key on, well clear of very
low frequency ambient noise.

Consequences: bit time is 1.0 second (a 12 bit frame takes about 12 seconds
on air), the surface decoder needs a bandpass filter tuned around 8 Hz plus
correlation against the known preamble, and emergency frames repeat 3 times
so a single noisy capture does not cost the whole message.

Superseded from the original plan: an earlier version of this decision
locked the carrier at 4 Hz. The final build uses 8 Hz, generated by flipping
the L298N driver's polarity through the coil at that rate.
