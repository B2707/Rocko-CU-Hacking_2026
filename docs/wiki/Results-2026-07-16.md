## Environmental caveat

The operator reported that people walked across the link during some frames.
Those frame identities were not logged, so this is a mixed clean/interference
dataset rather than a controlled duty-only sweep. Human motion can change the
magnetic channel and raise nonstationary noise; it may explain part of the
frame-to-frame variation and the measured 50% SNR exceeding 100%.

Keep the published all-frame results unchanged. If a clean subset is produced,
select it using predeclared signal-independent motion/interference criteria or
operator timestamps—not by removing frames that decoded incorrectly. Motion
frames should be retained as a separate robustness/stress benchmark.

## Simplified group-codebook pipeline

The unrestricted 65,536-message SLNN was replaced experimentally by four
independent 16-entry Hamming(7,4) codebook decisions. Because the complete code
is a direct product of four groups and the LLR score is additive, this is
mathematically equivalent to the unrestricted full-message argmax. It produced
zero decision mismatches against that decoder across all 30 frames with both
manifest timing and locally searched timing.

| Duty | Known timing | Local tilde-peak timing | Peak >= 0.8 |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 100% | 6/6 | 6/6 | 5/6 |
| 50% | 6/6 | 6/6 | 5/6 |
| 25% | 6/6 | 6/6 | 2/6 |
| 10% | 0/6 | 0/6 | 0/6 |
| 1% | 0/6 | 0/6 | 0/6 |

Thus the simplified non-RNN pipeline matches the previous coherent SLNN at
18/30 decoded frames while using only four 16-word group searches. With the
current 0.8 preamble threshold it accepts 12/30.

## LLR ablation

Using identical timing, channel estimates, covariance weights, and group
codebooks, the signed coherent LLR was compared with phase-discarding matched
amplitude differences and hard decisions.

| Duty | Signed coherent LLR | Matched amplitude, no LLR | Hard amplitude bits |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 100% | 6/6 | 6/6 | 6/6 |
| 50% | 6/6 | 6/6 | 6/6 |
| 25% | 6/6 | 4/6 | 4/6 |
| 10% | 0/6 | 0/6 | 0/6 |
| 1% | 0/6 | 0/6 | 0/6 |
| Total | 18/30 | 16/30 | 16/30 |

The held-out result fell from 3/5 to 2/5 without the signed coherent LLR; the
25% H frame was lost. Known and locally searched timing produced identical
counts. If “without LLR” means retaining the signed coherent statistic
`Re{h^H R^-1(z_first-z_second)}` but omitting only its factor of two, decisions
are mathematically identical: that statistic is already proportional to the
LLR. The observed loss occurs when phase/sign information is discarded.

## Important synchronization qualification

At 25%, coherent codeword decoding recovered 6/6 at manifest-aligned starts,
but four preamble scores were below the live 0.8 threshold. Therefore this is
not 6/6 autonomous detection. Acquisition/H0 rejection is now the principal
bottleneck near the sensitivity limit.
